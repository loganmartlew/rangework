# Rangework: Brand Strategy

---

## Positioning Statement

For serious amateur golfers who want their range time to actually improve their game, **Rangework** is the practice planning app that turns aimless ball-hitting into structured, repeatable sessions you can build at home and execute at the range. Rangework focuses on what happens _before_ a round, because the range is where scores are made.

---

## Target Audience

### Primary: The Intentional Practiser

Mid-to-low handicap golfers (roughly 5–18) who already know _what_ they need to work on and want a system to make sure they actually do it. They think about practice the way an athlete thinks about training: with structure, progression, and purpose. They're the type who writes drills on the back of a scorecard or keeps a note on their phone, frustrated that no tool exists to do it properly.

**Demographics:** 25–50, smartphone-native, willing to pay for tools that save time and sharpen performance. Skews male but not exclusively. Plays 1–3 times per week, practices at least as often.

**Psychographics:** Competitive but self-directed. Reads instruction content, watches swing videos, and thinks about their game between rounds. Values efficiency. They have limited time at the range and don't want to waste it deciding what to hit next.

### Secondary: The Coached Player

Golfers working with a teaching pro or following an online programme who need a way to capture and execute assigned drills between lessons. The coach says "do these three things before we meet again" and the player needs somewhere to put them.

### Tertiary: The Aspiring Competitor

Junior, college, and early-career competitive players building disciplined practice habits. Often following a coach's programme but beginning to design their own sessions as they mature.

---

## Value Proposition

### Core Promise

**Know exactly what you're doing before you get to the range, and follow through when you're there.**

### Supporting Pillars

1. **Build once, use forever.** Design drills with step-by-step instructions, ball counts, club assignments, and mental cues. Reuse them across any number of sessions without re-entering anything.

2. **Plan the full session.** Combine drills into complete practice templates with repeat counts, club overrides, and a live ball count total. You'll know what you're signing up for before you leave the house.

3. **Execute with focus.** Launch a live range session from any template and work through each step one at a time. Tap to complete, auto-advance to the next. No scrolling through notes, no trying to remember what comes after wedges.

4. **Pick up where you left off.** Progress saves continuously. Close the app, take a call, grab water. Your session is waiting exactly where you left it.

## Homepage Messaging Hierarchy

The homepage should move visitors through five psychological stages, each answered by a section. The overall arc is: _recognition → reframing → mechanism → proof → action._

| Priority | Visitor Question        | Message Goal                                                                                                          |
| -------- | ----------------------- | --------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| 1        | "Is this for me?"       | Create instant recognition: _you already practise with intention, and this is the tool you've been doing it without._ |
| 2        | "Why does this matter?" | Reframe the problem: most golf tech is about the round, but the range is where scores are actually built.             |
| 3        | "How does it work?"     | Show the three-level system (drills → sessions → live execution) as dead-simple.                                      |
| 4        | "Can I trust it?"       | Demonstrate the product in action: screenshots, a walkthrough, or a sample session that feels real.                   |
| 5        | "What do I do next?"    | Single clear CTA: download from Google Play.                                                                          |

### 2. The Problem (Reframe)

**Section title:** You've got the tools. You're missing the plan.

**Copy:**
You have a launch monitor. A swing app. A GPS watch. A stat tracker. But when you show up at the range, you're still standing over a pile of balls trying to remember what your coach said, what you worked on last time, and whether you should hit driver or wedges first.

The gap is structure. Rangework fills it.

---

### 3. How It Works (Three-step walkthrough)

**Section title:** Build drills. Plan sessions. Execute at the range.

**Step 1: Design your drills**
Create reusable practice units with step-by-step instructions, ball counts, club assignments, notes, and a mental focus cue. A drill might be "50-yard pitch shots: three landing zones, 10 balls each."

**Step 2: Compose your session**
Combine drills into a complete practice template. Set repeat counts, override clubs per slot, and watch the ball count total update in real time. You'll know exactly how many balls you need before you leave the house.

**Step 3: Execute on the range**
Hit "Start" and Rangework turns your session into a guided, step-by-step flow. One instruction at a time. Tap to complete, auto-advance to the next. Close the app and come back; your progress is saved to the exact step.

**Visual:** Three sequential phone screens: drill editor, session builder with ball count, live range session with a step in focus.

---

### 4. Key Benefits (Feature highlights)

**Section title:** Built for the range.

- **Ball count planning.** See the total ball count for any session before you go. No more guessing whether you'll need a medium or large bucket.
- **Your club bag, your rules.** Enable only the clubs you carry from a catalogue of 30. Every dropdown in the app reflects your actual bag.
- **Step-by-step execution.** Work through each instruction one at a time during a live range session. No scrolling, no re-reading, no forgetting where you were.
- **Continuous save.** Progress persists after every tap. Walk away, come back, pick up exactly where you left off.
- **Cloud sync.** Your drills and sessions are tied to your account and available on any device.
- **Time tracking.** Rangework records how long you spend on the range automatically, so you can review your practice habits over time.

## Brand Voice Guidelines (for homepage and beyond)

**Tone:** Direct, confident, specific. Write like a good playing partner: someone who knows what they're talking about and doesn't waste words. No hype, no jargon, no "revolutionise your game."

**Vocabulary:**

- Use "drill" and "session" over "workout" or "routine" (those sound like fitness, not golf).
- Use "ball count" over "volume" or "reps" (ball count is how golfers already think about it).
- Use "range" and "practice" over "training" (training is coach-speak, not player-speak).
- Use "build" and "plan" over "create" or "design" (build implies intention; create is generic).

**Things to avoid:**

- Claiming the app will lower your handicap. It structures practice; the improvement is on the golfer.
- Comparing to other golf apps. Rangework occupies its own category entirely.
- Over-featuring. Lead with the _problem_ and the _workflow_. Features support the story; they don't tell it.
